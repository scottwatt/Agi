WATER DAMAGE RESTORATION

Did you know that according to The Canadian Institute of Actuaries, the water damage was responsible for 48% of all home insurance claims. Flooding can occur any time. It can happen to anyone who has a basement, even if never flooded before. While most often flooding occurs during big rains or rapid snow-melts in the spring, it can occur even during dry weather.  In addition to the damage caused to your belongings, flooded basements can also be sources of high concentrations of mold if not addressed quickly. That is why if you discovered an unpleasant surprise in the face of ankle-deep brown water in your basement, or on your unit floor, on Sunday - you are not alone!  When your basement is flooded and your precious belongings are floating - don't wait and take action immediately! Call ORBIS at 1-844-ORBIS-24.

Our IICRC certified water damage restoration technicians are available 24/7. We will respond and provide a detailed inspection that includes pictures of the flooded basement in order to thoroughly document the damages for the insurance claims. Our technicians will also begin the process to immediately start extracting any standing water. It is much more efficient to remove liquid water than it is to dry it up. Under the extraction methods, specialized “water vacuums” and similar devices are used to suck up moisture that might remain on cushions, carpets, or other such items. This form of water extraction can go a long way towards salvaging materials that would otherwise be compromised.

Structural drying is the next major step after water extraction. While extraction can remove any standing water, it doesn’t do so well when it comes to the moisture left behind on your property. Structural drying is used to air out, dehumidify, and dry up any lingering wetness in order to eliminate the chance of future problems caused by moisture. Our certified by IICRC field experts will identify the class of water damage to ensure we execute the best process to mitigate any secondary damages.

There are two main types of structural drying methods we use:

  • Air Flow/Evaporation:  High-velocity air is used to dry out any moisture that cannot be extracted. High-powered air movers are strategically spaced and angled to get maximum coverage so no water can escape. Air flow may be heated as required to improve the evaporation process.
  • Dehumidification: Dehumidifiers are used to balance the environment of your property and resolve the problem when moisture is re-absorbed. It significantly speeds up the structural drying process but also serves as another way to restrict any possible mold growths.
